Hello

About

"Hello" is my ITP thesis project. "Hello" is a technological companion that sits on a user's desk and prints a selection from their Gmail chat archive once a day. The piece addresses our constant interaction with technology, and is meant to raise questions concerning the function of memory and the utility of remembrance in a world where our every action is recorded and logged.

"Hello" was written in Python, using a combination of Flask and Heroku to output chat conversations online. A wireless Arduino then made an HTTP request to get the chat conversation and send it serially to the printer. I bought the box in Texas as a decorative object, and covered the inside with a piece of copper that I cut to size and then treated. "Hello" is meant to live innocuously among everyday objects on my desk, intermittently printing out a conversation that serves as a reminder of the past.
