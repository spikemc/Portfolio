MPW64: Two Days On

Lincoln County Fire Protection District's Station No. 1 in Troy, Mo., is the only 24 hour manned fire station in the county. Covering 160 square miles of farmland, city and forest, the firemen from the station responded to 1,500 calls last year and extinguished 350 fires.

Firefighters work a "two-day-on, four-day-off" schedule. During those two days (and nights) they live at the firehouse together, training, doing maintenance, working with volunteers and attending to their families and lives as best they can. The calls run the gamut from medical emergencies to structure fires to car accidents to catastrophic floods. For 48 hours each week they work and live together to protect and aid Lincoln County residents.

Firefighters Allen Heyer, Lee Schieffer and Steven Fair joke while performing a mandatory end-of-shift cleaning on the final morning of two-day shift.

Captain Mike Noonan gives a lesson in fire safety to a pre-k class. Class visits are common at the fire station, and classes often drop by to give the children a glimpse of firehouse life.

A two car accident outside of Troy that left two women with non-life threatening injuries. Firefighters lend assistance in anyway they can, in this case stabilizing victims, driving ambulances, assisting police and clearing the road after the accident.

Firefighter Lee Schieffer grabs a medical kit while responding to an early morning medical call in a trailer park in Troy.

Volunteer firefighter Cleveland Zinn, 18 watches intently during a live-fire training exercise. Eighty-five percent of fire departments in the US rely on volunteer firefighters to help fill the gaps, and Lincoln County is no exception.

About Missouri Photo Workshop

The Missouri Photo Workshop has been documenting life in small town Missouri since being founded by Cliff Edom in 1949. For one week each year, photographers inhabit, learn, and tell the stories of a different small town in Missouri. The workshop still holds true to Cliff Edom's credo: "Show truth with a camera. Ideally truth is a matter of personal integrity. In no circumstances will a posed or faked photograph be tolerated."

I attended the 64th Missouri Photo Workshop in 2012, and spent a week with the firefighters of Lincoln County Fire Protection Station No. 1
