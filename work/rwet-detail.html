Reading and Writing Electronic Text

Reading and Writing Electronic Text is a class taught at ITP by Adam Parrish. From the class website:

This course introduces the Python programming language as a tool for reading and writing digital text. This course is specifically geared to serve as a general-purpose introduction to programming in Python, but will be of special interest to students interested in poetics, language, creative writing and text analysis.

For my final, I took the works of playwright Charles Mee and re-imagined them using Python. Mee worked with found and reconstituted texts, and so manipulating his texts seemed appropriate. I ended up with a few pieces, created using markov chains.

Finally, I'm interested in remixing the works of Mee and performing them. I repeatedly ran a selection of text through a Markov chain, alternating the length until I ended up with the following text. I initially assigned two "characters" to read, and added another "character" every time the text suggest another person entered the scene. I would like to continue playing with text this way to create a programmatic scene.
