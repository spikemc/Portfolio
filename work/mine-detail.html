MINEITSMINE

MINEITSMINE is a location based game created by myself, Paul May and Natalie Be'er for ITP's Big Games class in Spring 2011.

MINEITSMINE originated from an idea of mine about how to turn New York City in to a large game board. We worked as a group to pare down the original idea to work within the constraints presented by the class: the game had to take place within the Greenwich Village loft space that ITP occupies within NYU's Tisch School of the Arts, and had to be playable within a short time.

About

The game centered around users "owning" space by discovering tokens hidden within that space. Using a laser cutter, we created simple masonite "star" tokens etched with the name of the room they were hidden in (for example "Classroom 50") and hot glued magnets to the backside. We also created a "crown" token with the name of the corresponding room etched on it. If players came to possess all the "Classroom 50" star tokens, they would receive the "Classroom 50" crown token. If one player possessed 4 of 5 star tokens, they could "challenge" the player that possessed the remaining token. Challenges consisted of simple competitions like the "do the most push-ups in one minute".

The game was to be played from 9am until class time at 3.30pm. The class was able to follow the progress of the game through a scoreboard drawn on a white board in the middle of ITP. As players found the tokens, they put them up next to their name on the board.
